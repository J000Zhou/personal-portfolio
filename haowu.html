Haowu - Online Shopping Price Tracking App

Challenge

To design and develop a price tracking application that simplifies the process of price comparisons during online shopping and minimise time spent on filtering out unreliable vendors.

Role

UX Designer responsible for research, UI design, iteration and implementation.

Team Members

Alec Madill & Sining Bao

Background

The project was inspired by online shoppers that spent a majority of their time jumping between multiple retailing websites to find a better deal. Users could be able to find a product with the best price on a reliable website more efficiently if there was a service built for their needs to create an overall better online shopping experience that helps save their time. Haowu is a service that proposes to enable users to maximise their online shopping efficiency by allowing them to simultaneously track items and prices across multiple vendors for the best deals.

*This is a fictional project.

Research

Secondary research was conducted to gain a foundational level of understanding of online shopping and marketing. According to the results, it was found that the Coronavirus pandemic had accelerated ecommerce growth and exponentially increased the sales of online products. It was also found to have an impact on consumer behaviour; consumers have shifted to digital channels for their purchasing needs and are likely to retain this behaviour succeeding the pandemic.

Subsequently, five user interviews were conducted with users that possessed online shopping experience to obtain a better understanding of their goals and difficulties buying products online. The interviews focused on understanding user demographics and online shopping behaviours.

Competitor Analysis

Following the analysis of the existing market, a competitor analysis was conducted on alternate retailing channels' strengths, weaknesses and available features. A majority of products were found to have implemented a notification system for price updated, including eBay, Amazon and Kmart.

User Interview Results

Based on interview results, the action of finding the best deal for a product was very time consuming as users had to manually compare prices across multiple online retailers.

3/5 Users compared prices of a single item from multiple websites before considering purchasing.

2/5 Users were pleased with the transparency of the prices and sale amounts of online items.

3/5 Users didn't trust prices listed on Google, believing for them to be out of date or inaccurate

Empathy Map

This empathy map draws out unexpected insights revolving around users' needs, based on the observations gathered from the interview data.

Persona

A persona was created to identify the target users based on the collected research data.
Sam frequently shops online and prefers a simple and fast experience with a high standard of quality.

Define

Problem Statement

Users find themselves frustrated when spending too much time manually comparing prices, finding sales and coupons and avoiding fake and unreliable websites while trying to find the best deals for their desired products while online shopping.

A platform that identifies the up-to-date pricings of user desired products from trustworthy online vendors will allow users to increase their shopping productivity and create a reassuring online shopping experience.

Storyboard

A storyboard was created to shape the user persona and accumulate product requirements.

User Journey Map

The user's journey is broken down into stages, each analysing the determined potential opportunities that could help solve the persona's frustrations and concerns.

Product Roadmap

Following the brainstorming of potential solutions for the service, features were selected and prioritised based on impact, cost, ease of implementation and time constraints. There were two main areas focus:

  • Latest prices from various retailers to be visible from one page
  • Users should be able to purchase an item at sale price until the end of the sale period

User Flow

Structure of the app was determined based on problem statement and product roadmap.

Ideate

Low-Fidelity Sketches

Ideas were sketched out on paper, with different ideas being explored and further refined. This process was conducted alongside competitors' mobile design patterns as a reference.

Low-Fidelity Wireframes

User Testing Feedback

Wireframes were tested against 3 participants to identify any difficulties when using the current design layout. It was observed that all users were able to complete their assigned tasks but had found it difficult to recognise interactive links and buttons. Users also mentioned that the content found in product cards was hard to follow.

Low-Fidelity Iterations

Wireframes were revised following user testing feedback, where CTAs were introduced to highlight important interactive elements and content held within product cards was reorganised to highlight the product image and group texts together to improve readability.

High-Fidelity Wireframes

Further development and refining of wireframes into a high-fidelity stage was completed based on user recommendations. Green was the chosen colour for the CTAs as it symbolises inviting, friendly and approachable themes, which will result in making the interactive elements a more obvious part of the UI design.

Next Steps

If given more time, it would be beneficial to conduct another round of usability testing to validate the buying process and identify difficulties when shifting to third parties for checkout. Development of this app into a responsive service would also be an advantageous decision, allowing users to access the service through multiple digital platforms.

Reflection

The Haowu mobile app as a UX design project was an amazing learning journey to experience. It was enlightening to observe how testing participants offered positive and constructive feedback on the incorporated design features and how they were eager to follow along on how the app would be deployed as a real-world product.
